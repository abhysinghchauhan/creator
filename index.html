M&A From the Inside

Market Understanding

The headline number looks massive: global M&A deal value is already ~$1.8 trillion in 2026 YTD. But for GrowthPal, the real serviceable market is mid-market to enterprise — deals typically in the $50M–$100M range — where acquisition mandates are active, Corp Dev teams are lean, and sourcing is still largely manual. Deloitte consistently flags mid-market M&A as underpenetrated but growing, particularly as more companies build dedicated inorganic growth functions.

Egnyte is a real example: their Corp Dev function — a three-person team led by the COO — runs a standing mandate to surface one acquisition-worthy target ($50–100M range) for leadership every two weeks. It's a year-long continuous process, and they're not shopping for software. They're looking for someone to reliably surface the right opportunity at the right moment. That buyer profile exists in scale across the US, Europe, and India — and GrowthPal is built precisely for them.

"My first priority will be finding a repeatable deal origination motion where we lead with actual acquisition opportunities, not just product demos. The moment a Corp Dev head or CEO sees a relevant off-market target in their inbox, the conversation changes entirely. Once that motion is working and proven, I'll scale the team around it. AEs, BDRs, regional hires — everything follows proof of repeatability, not assumption."

"Product training is not a one-time onboarding task — it's a continuous process that runs in parallel with every phase. Understanding the platform deeply allows me to translate features into real business outcomes for enterprise buyers. Strong product fluency is what makes that translation possible, and it sharpens with every customer conversation."

01

This phase is pure intelligence gathering — and I'll be in the room for as much of it as possible. I'll attend every customer-facing call, especially early in the sales cycle, where the real buying motivations and objections surface. The goal: walk into Day 31 with a clear diagnosis of what's working, what's broken, and where the biggest pipeline opportunity lies across defined markets.

Week 2

Run discovery calls with existing clients — Razorpay, Quantiphi, Mindtree profile types across regions. Understand why they bought, what they valued, and what friction they faced during the process.

Week 3

Conduct 5 lost-deal post-mortems. Review competitor positioning — Grata, Inven, SourceScrub, PitchBook. Map where GrowthPal wins and loses on value. Note the Grata/SourceScrub Datasite acquisition as a live displacement opportunity.

Week 4

Synthesise all findings. Deliver a written audit to the founding team: ICP clarity, target countries, penetration strategy, pipeline health across regions, and the top 3 structural gaps to fix in Phase 2.

03

AEs will be hired once the funnel has consistent demo volume that justifies the cost. We scale after proving repeatability, not before.

Run first structured weekly deal review. Confirm 2–3 accounts are in active, qualified conversations. Refine the playbook based on real data — what's getting replies, where demos stall, and where we're winning on value.

Week 12

Present the 90-day report to founders: pipeline health, conversion metrics, ICP refinements, learnings from US vs EU vs India buyer behaviour, and a 6-month roadmap — including the conditions and evidence base for when to hire the first AE.
